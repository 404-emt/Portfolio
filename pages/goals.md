My long-term goal is to pursue a PhD in robotics, control, or a related field, and 
contribute to research at the intersection of theoretical foundations and practical 
autonomous systems. I am not yet certain of the exact subfield, but I know that more 
research experience is the clearest path to finding out — and to being recognized as 
a serious candidate.

## Long-Term Vision

Become a research scientist or professor working on autonomous systems, machine learning, control theory, 
or robotics — contributing both to academic knowledge and to real-world engineering.

## Milestones & Timeline

| Period | Goal |
|---|---|
| Year 1 (2025–2026) | Join a research lab, build foundational experience |
| Year 2 (2026–2027) | Continue research, prepare graduate school applications |
| Year 3 (2027–2028) | BS/MS program if applicable, or transition to PhD |
| Post-graduation | Doctoral study, predoctoral research, or research internship |

## Steps to Get There

To prepare for PhD programs in robotics or control, I plan to:

1. **Build sustained research experience** — through Georgia Tech's ORS/VIP program 
   and ECE research opportunities. I am currently applying to ORS and actively looking 
   for faculty whose work aligns with my interests in control and autonomous systems.

2. **Deepen theoretical foundations** — by taking advanced coursework in control theory, 
   optimization, and machine learning within my Computer Engineering concentrations 
   (Distributed Systems and Robotics & Autonomous Systems).

3. **Pursue publication opportunities** — building on my existing ICRA co-authorship 
   to develop an independent research track record before applying to doctoral programs.

4. **Gain professional research exposure** — through predoctoral research internships 
   at universities or industry research labs during summers, continuing the trajectory 
   established through my existing external research experience.

5. **Build faculty relationships** — by working closely with professors at Georgia Tech 
   whose research I admire, with the goal of securing strong letters of recommendation 
   and research mentorship for graduate school applications.
